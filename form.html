Service Report












A risk assessment needs to be carried out on the activity before any work commences. If there is an annual risk assessment in place for this job section A forms part of the control measures that MUST be implemented to reduce risks to acceptable levels. Failure to implement these standards may lead to personal injuries and, as they are based on legislation and/or best practice.



Point of Work Risk Assessment

Hazards or risks present on site


Have you signed in at site and made your presence known to your site contact?

Have you completed the site permit to work?


Have you contacted the Fleetsolve office and agreed the next contact time?

Do you have safe access to and from the CHP and plant room?

Do you have sufficient illumination and equipment for the outlined task?

Is the unit currently visually in a safe condition?

Are your hand tools safe and adequate for the task?

Is your test equipment safe and in within calibration?

Are the manual handling requirements with the task within your capabilities?

Do you have adequate and appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) available?

Are you aware of the precautions needed when working with hazardous substances? (COSHH)

Do you have adequate and appropriate spillage equipment available?

Will you ensure that all environmental contamination is reported?

Will you ensure you conduct your work in a safe manner where so far as is reasonably practicable and report any accidents/incidents or near misses?



If you answer No to any of the previous questions, you must ensure you resolve the problem with the Site Contact and ensure you inform the Fleetsolve Office (do this even if the local Site Management can resolve the issue safely and effectively).
